Opening Credits

The opening credits animations were achieved using the title animations available in Ulead VideoStudio. The circular background behind the text was created in Adobe Photoshop. For the high-resolution version of the film, the full 30 fps remains as exported by VideoStudio.

Some special things needed to be done to get these animations into The Movies as overlays. Because these were transparent, I created two separated projects in VideoStudio. The first project contains the text and circular backdrop with the appropriate colours, with a black background. The second project was an exact copy, but changed the text to pure white, and the circular backdrop to a very light grey. Once these projects were exported as AVI files, they were brought into Photoshop and combined frame-by-frame. The correspond frame from the first project made up the RGB channel, while the second project made up the alpha channel.

Once I had a big list of overlays, I had to do some creative things to rename the overlays for use with the film. This sequence in the film uses a new technique that requires transparent animated overlays to cross multiple shots. To achieve this, you will have to re-think how you attach your overlays to each shot in Advanced Movie Maker.

Normally, you create an animation and attach it to a single scene. Here, you must create one long animation (or multiple animations that play back-to-back), and then split them up according to how you have edited your shots. Follow these steps:

  • Finalize the sequence of shots in Advanced Movie Maker, and get them all in the proper sequence.
  • Attach a single transparent overlay frame to each scene in AMM. Name them something along the lines of "overlay_cast01_v00.dds", "overlay_cast02_v00.dds", etc. (The exact names are up to you - it's a good idea to include some abbreviation of your film's title.) You will be turning these into animated overlays later. The important thing is to get them attached to the scenes.
  • The first time you do this, you may want to make a backup copy of your film now and run through the rest of the steps - just to get some practice in.
  • Once your film is complete in AMM, take it into Post Production and edit the sequence. You must complete the editing before you continue any further.
  • Take careful note of the exact length, in tenths-of-a-second, of every shot in the sequence.
  • Make copies of the transparent animated time-test overlays and rename them to "overlay_cast##_v##.dds". Each consecutive animated overlay must have the same number of frames as there are tenths-of-a-second in the shot. A shot that is exactly 1.0 second will therefore have 10 frames, numbered v00 through v09.
  • Go back into your film. This time, take careful note of the frame number at the beginning of every shot in the sequence.
  • Now you will finally rename your actual overlay files to "overlay_cast##_v##.dds". Start the naming with the first frame number of the first shot. Each consecutive frame in your animation follows along:
    • Consecutively increase the numbering of the v## portion.
    • "Wrap around" the numbering in the same animated overlay until all v##'s are filled.
    • Move to the next cast## overlay, starting at the v## from your notes.
